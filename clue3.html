Facing Obstacles

Fun Fact:

In 1972, Ugandan dictator Idi Amin expelled the entire Asian community, stripping citizenship from people who had lived there for generations. This single decree rewrote the country's economy, its identity, and still echoes today—proving that deciding "who belongs" can reshape a nation overnight.

Question to Ponder:

Think about how exclusion doesn't merely affect those who are left out, but also reverberates through every sphere of the nation.
