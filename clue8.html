Fun Fact:

Did you know a father can be "symbolic"? In the author's childhood, growing up in a polygamous household meant sharing dad's love with three homes and many siblings. But it was mom—who sold clothes from a tiny Kampala stall—who became the unshakable pillar of love, teaching her kids that determination matters more than comfort.

Question to Ponder:

How does having both parents present in your childhood shape your identity as an adult?
